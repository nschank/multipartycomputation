Welcome

Multiparty Computation is a complex, poorly-explained field in Computer Science and Cryptography. Within these pages, we hope to explain to researchers, students, enthusiasts, and anyone else interested what multiparty computation is, how it can be used to improve their lives, and what they can do to help. To get started, follow a link below, or mouse over the dark green line above to open the menu.

Getting Started

This site was carefully built to be accessible by people from any level of background in Computer Science or Cryptography. Find below wherever you think would be best to start:

Beginners

There is a comprehensive tutorial section built just for people who have never really dealt with Cryptography before.

Students

The tutorial section contains a large amount of information, including examples and code samples. If you need to dive further in depth, use the citations or go directly to the research section in order to find more advanced topics.

Companies

If you're looking for an implementation or for an idea of how Multiparty Computation can help you or your business, the example library is a great place to start.

Researchers

Stand on the shoulders of giants by seeing our catalogue of Multiparty Computation research here. Further inspiration can be found in the next steps section where we look at what things are yet to be done in the field.

Help Out

Are you a researcher in the field or knowledgeable about Multiparty Computation? If so, you can help in our mission to spread the word and expand the field! If you're interested in doing so then, please, help us out by:

  • Submitting your research or thesis to be added to our research section. More information on how to submit your own work can be found here.
  • Checking our work! If you find any mistakes, there is a link on every page to tell us about it.
  • Suggesting a section or page, or even writing it yourself and submitting it.
  • Going out and adding to our knowledge!
