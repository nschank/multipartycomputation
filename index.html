Welcome

Multiparty Computation is a field of Computer Science that allows a group of people to each individually keep their data secret, while cooperatively calculating information about that data. It is an extremely useful process that has applications for voting, auctions, better privacy on the web, and dozens of other measures that we currently do non-optimally. Despite this usability, the field remains underused and esoteric. Within these pages, we hope to explain to researchers, students, enthusiasts, and anyone else interested, what multiparty computation is, how it can be useful, and what they can do to help. Getting Started

This site was carefully built to be accessible by people from any level of background in Computer Science or Cryptography. Find below wherever you think would be best to start:

Beginners

There is a comprehensive tutorial section built just for people who have never really dealt with Cryptography before.

Students

The tutorial section contains a large amount of information, including examples and code samples. If you need to dive further in depth, use the citations or go directly to the research section in order to find more advanced topics.

Companies

Help Out

Are you a researcher in the field or knowledgeable about Multiparty Computation? If so, you can help in our mission to spread the word and expand the field outside of academia! If you're interested in doing so then, please, help us out by:

  • Submitting your research or thesis to be added to our research section. You can do so by directly contributing to our GitHub or by emailing us your paper.
  • Checking our work! If you find any mistakes, there is a link on every page to tell us about it.
  • Suggesting a section or page, or even writing it yourself and submitting it.
  • Going out and adding to our knowledge!
